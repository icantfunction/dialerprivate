CFG · Million Dollar Script
Widow-of-Veteran outreach · Live call playbook
01 Intro
"Hello (CLIENT NAME), this is YOUR NAME with the Benefits Office.

How are you doing today?

I got your request, it looks like you wanted me to go over your entitlements for being a Widow of a Veteran. You are in (STATE) — is that correct?"
PAUSE
"Perfect, I am the Underwriter assigned to your File. That is going to help you with all the benefits you are entitled to!

Were you looking for something specific? How can I assist you today?"
Agent noteFor-agents-only intro objections live in the Objections section at the bottom.
02 Build Rapport & Discovery
"Okay, I will check on all the benefits you are entitled to, so you don't miss out on anything that is available for Widows of Veterans."
  • Are you looking more into medical or financial help?
  • Are you currently getting any benefits?
SoftenAsk about kids, weather, anything human — keep it loose before pivoting.
03 Profile Verification
"Okay, thank you for that information."
PAUSE for the next question
"Let me pull up your profile details and see what you may qualify for.

May I have your ZIP code? And your date of birth?"
LET THEM REACT
"Perfect, I was able to find you here in the system."
"If you don't mind me asking, when did your husband pass away?"
"God bless you, ma'am. When he passed, did you have to come out of pocket for anything?"
"I see. Well ma'am, I see here that those benefits will transfer over to you. If, God forbid, something happens to you, they will take care of your burial."
LET THEM REACT
"Yeah, because if you get this covered outside of your entitlements they're going to charge you hundreds of dollars. But if you use your entitlements, it's going to cost you a lot less."
"Yes, you can get that taken care of through your entitlements. Do you have anything in place to cover the burial?"
04 If They Have Coverage
"That's great to hear!"
"Was that coverage set up through the VA?"
They will always say no.
  • "How come? Is it a Term Life?"
  • "How much is the coverage? For how long have you had it?"
"Perfect. Ma'am, are you willing to have your (beneficiary / family) use the life insurance money to cover the burial, or would you like to just use your entitlements to cover everything?"
"So the coverage you have now would be extra money you're leaving behind for your family, and your entitlements will cover the burial. That way your family doesn't have to use the life insurance money."
If they say noContinue straight into the qualification questions below.
"To determine your eligibility, I will ask you some questions about your income and some medical questions."
05 Income Qualification
  • "Now, are you currently working, retired, or disabled?"
  • If retired: "I can't wait to say that one day!"
  • If disabled: "Bless your heart!"
  • "What would you say you bring in per month, just to the ballpark?"
  • "Is there anyone at the house depending on you financially?" (skip if already mentioned)
  • "At the end of the month, once you pay all your bills, utilities, and all the fun things you like to do — how much would you say you are typically left with?"
Income Objection "Why do you need to know that?"
"The benefits that you are going to be entitled to depend on how much income is left over."
06 Start the Application
"Thank you, ma'am. I'll go ahead and fill out this request for you. Let's start with your address. What is a good address for you?"
WorkflowWalk through the application form fields — except SSN — then proceed to height/weight and medical.
"Okay, thank you, ma'am. Now we are going to move on to the medical side of things. My mom would hate me if I asked you this, but what is a good height and weight for you?"
"Gotcha! They're going to pull your medical records now — they're going to look at medications and doctors you're seeing to determine your eligibility, okay?"
Get an "okay" from them.
"Okay, now they're going to pull your medical records. What is your social?"

Social Security Objection

Push Back #1 "I completely understand. Now, the main reason they ask for that piece of information is that's how they can identify and know you're first and last name for the medical information bureau. Does that make sense? Go ahead with your social, please."
Push Back #2 "I completely understand. Most importantly, that's the ONLY thing on your death certificate when you die for them to pay out your loved ones. Does that make sense? Go ahead with your social, please."
07 Medical Questions
"Got it. Now I'm going to ask you some medical questions."
"Now, for all of your medical needs, do you go to the VA or a civilian doctor? Are all of your prescriptions prescribed through the VA or a civilian doctor?"
If yes"Have you ever been rushed to a non-VA hospital for an emergency? What was that for?"
  • Any heart attacks, heart failure, strokes, TIA, or stents in the last 5 years?
    • If yes: Are you currently on any blood thinners or heart medications?
    • Blood thinners: Plavix or warfarin?
    • Heart meds: Nitrostat, nitroglycerin, Eliquis?
  • Any cancer in the last 5 years? What kind? How long in remission (cancer-free)?
  • Any diabetes? If yes: Are you on metformin or insulin?
  • Any neuropathy? If yes: Are you taking gabapentin?
  • Any high blood pressure? If yes: Are you taking lisinopril, metoprolol, or amlodipine?
  • Any breathing complications or COPD? If yes: Are you on oxygen or an inhaler?
  • Any anxiety or depression? If yes: Are you taking Prozac or Seroquel?
  • Any kidney or liver problems? If kidney: any failure / disorder / dialysis?
  • Any hospitalizations in the last year for 48 hours or more?
  • One last thing — a rough height and weight for you?
08 Benefits Presentation
"Perfect, ma'am. It looks like you are entitled to a lot. Please get a pen and paper so you can write them down."
Agent ruleFocus on what value brings to them, not your value. Pick 4 features — and ALWAYS include Living Benefits. By this point you should be on the bank-info page of the app.
Immediate coverage: "As soon as you make your first contribution you're covered day 1 — no 2-year wait period like most carriers!"
Locked in: "Premium never increases and coverage never decreases!"
Tax-free: "The death benefit, living benefit, and cash value are one of the few things we don't have to pay Uncle Sam for!" (chuckle)
Living benefit: "This one is important — IF you get a terminal illness and the doctor tells you that you have a few years to live, you'll have access to 100% of the benefit tax-free while you're still living! Isn't that awesome, (CLIENT NAME)?"
Cash value: "Your policy will accumulate cash value over time."
Double Accidental Payout: "If your cause of death is choke, drown, slip, fall, or a car accident, your coverage would double. It's an accidental rider built into your policy."
Permanent coverage: "Lastly, this coverage will never expire on you — it is a whole-life policy."
09 Quote & Lock the Premium
Pricing referenceBurial $7,000 – $15,000 · Cremation $2,000 – $5,000. Always quote higher to lower if needed.
"Do you want to cover a burial or cremation?"
"This will cover the full burial / cremation and make sure (BENEFICIARY NAME) is taken care of — they don't have to pay for anything. It also includes a ceremony and provides a cushion for any income loss.

It was supposed to cost you $XXX, but since you are a Widow of a Veteran your contribution will only be $XXX…"
Continue to GREEN — no pause. "Now that sounds a lot better."
Push Back #1 — "Oh, that's too much" "Now the only thing left to do is to see if you are healthy enough to get approved today."
"Perfect. Now we'll send in a request for coverage and hope to get the approval. Keep in mind they don't approve of everyone, but I will do my best to get you the coverage, okay?"
10 Banking · Immediate Draft
Use only if leftover income is high ($500+)"If the policy is to be approved, would you like to be covered right away like most Widows?"

How to ask for banking

"Okay perfect, we'll set up the initial payment (on the date you discussed)."
"Who do you bank with?"
Google that bank/state's routing number, read it off to them, then ask for the account #. Make them repeat it twice. Then: "Is this a checking or savings account?"
"Perfect — give me one moment while the system verifies that information is linked to your name, for your safety and the safety of others."

No checkbook path

  • Q1 — Do you get your statements via mail or email?
  • Q2 — Do you do online banking?
CHECKMATE — If they have no checkbook AND can't find a bank statement AND can't find the account #: "No worries, we can just add the debit card on file for now and update it later. Go ahead with the card you want to put on file." Ask Visa / Mastercard, write down card number, expiration month/year, CVV. Make them repeat it twice. Then pivot to AIG SIWL. Write down all info and proceed with the app.

Bank Objections

Push Back #1 "I completely understand. Now, quick question, (Client's Name) — have you ever given or received a check from anyone in your lifetime? Perfect. If you notice at the bottom of every check, you'll see the bank's routing and account number — because that information can't be used to buy something online or go to your local Walmart and go on a shopping spree, okay? What would be weird is if I'd asked you for something like a debit / credit card, which is an unsecured payment method. Does that make sense? Go ahead with your account number."
Push Back #2 — "Why do I have to give you that banking information now?" "I completely understand. The state is required by law to validate that the information provided is linked to your name, for your safety and the safety of others.

Do you receive text messages to this phone? I'm going to send you a picture of my screen for further transparency."
Send a pic of the application with carrier name and their name.
"Do you see your name there? And the name of the carrier on the screen? Now, when you are ready with that information…"
Have them do it!Or teach them how.
11 Locking Down the Close
"So (Client Name), I've got good news — everything has been approved and you're all set.

Just to recap real quick: the coverage is $XXX with XXX as the carrier. Your policy will arrive in the mail in about 10–12 business days.

The number we're speaking on right now is my direct line. If you ever have a question, need help, or just want something explained again, please don't hesitate to call or text me. That's exactly what I'm here for.

I also want to say this — the reason so many people choose to do this is because it brings peace of mind. You've taken a moment today to make sure your family won't be left guessing or struggling later on. That's a very loving and responsible thing to do, and it really does matter.

One small thing to be aware of: once a policy is approved, we notify the state Department of Insurance, which helps reduce unwanted calls about this coverage. The only people who should ever contact you about your policy are me or the insurance company. If anyone else calls and says something doesn't sound right, just give me a call — I'll take care of it for you.

Before we hang up — do you feel good about the decision you made today for yourself and your family?

I truly appreciate you trusting me, (Client Name). You did a good thing today. Enjoy the rest of your day, and please take care."
12 Phase I · Intro Objections
"Looking for benefits!"
"Perfect, sir. This is a new program as part of your Veteran Benefits that gives you access to an exclusive discounted rate for your final expense and life options. Do you currently have anything in place?"
Then go back to the script.
"When I pass away, the VA will take care of my burial." (Veterans often think they're buried for free.)
"I completely understand, but that's actually a common misconception. I wish that were the case — if it were up to me, all veterans would get put to rest at no cost! But unfortunately, too many veterans don't know what the VA actually covers when they pass away. The VA covers your plot, but your loved ones would be responsible for your burial / cremation.

That being said — God forbid something happened to you today, who would be responsible for the funeral expenses and picking up all the pieces tomorrow?"
Then go back to the script.

Most people's main concern when they fill this out is to make sure that their final expense doesn't fall as a burden on their loved ones, or to leave some extra money behind. Do you currently have anything in place?"
Then go back to the script.
"I'm not interested!"

If yes: Perfect — when you sent this in, were you just trying to save some money with the new discounted programs, or add more coverage?"
Then go back to the script.

Then go back to the script.

13 Phase II · Numbers Objections
"Can you send it in the mail?"
"I completely understand that you want it sent in the mail, but this isn't something where you see it, like it, and buy it. We would need to submit the request for coverage and hope to get the approval. So given these three options, which one makes the most sense for you — the gold, silver, or bronze?"
"I need to think about it!"
"I completely understand. Did you want to think about if you needed the coverage at all, or which coverage amount you should go with?"
If "which one" "Let's start with the bare minimum — make sure you can get the approval and foot in the door, and that it's comfortable and affordable. Then we can always increase it in the future. Does that make sense? So to get started on the app…"
Proceed to script.
If "don't need" "Spin back on why. Do they have any insurance? Any money saved up? If yes, is it enough?" (10k isn't enough for burial; most add 5k to factor in inflation.)

Challenge: "Do you mind if I make a recommendation?"

14 VA Programs · Agent Homework

Drop these into the conversation to build trust and demonstrate you know what's available for them.

  • ★VA Healthcare— medical coverage through VA facilities
  • ★Disability Compensation Annual Increase— yearly COLA bumps to monthly compensation
  • ★Aid & Attendance— monthly add-on for help with daily living
  • ★DIC— Dependency & Indemnity Compensation for survivors
  • ★CHAMPVA— health coverage for eligible spouses / children
  • ★VA Survivor Pension for Widows— income-based pension for low-income surviving spouses
